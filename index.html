        Hi, I'm Yih-Shiuan (易萱, aka 小黑)! Welcome to my personal website—I'm glad you found your way here!

        I'm a visual neuroscientist, and I use behavioral and neuroscience techniques to study human vision—how we see and understand the world around us.

        Originally from Taiwan, after receiving my master in the lab of Prof. Chien-Chung Chen (陳建中) in National Taiwan University, I earned my PhD in the lab of Prof. Mark Greenlee at the University of Regensburg, Germany. Currently, I am working as a postdoctoral researcher in Mark's lab. As our project nears its completion, I'm actively exploring opportunities for my next position.

        If you know of any opportunities for a visual neuroscientist with expertise in psychophysics, fMRI, and MEG, I'd love to hear from you!

        On this site, you'll find more about my research, along with some fascinating visual illusion demos.

        Have fun exploring! Feel free to contact me if you'd like to learn more about my work or connect further.
